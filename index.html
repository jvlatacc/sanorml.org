NORML is one of the largest and oldest non-profit
marijuana activist organizations in the world.

NORML supports the removal of all criminal penalties for the private possession and responsible use of marijuana by adults. Our local San Antonio NORML chapter promotes a better understanding of the medical, legal, environmental, and civil libertarian arguments for more reasonable treatments of cannabis laws in Texas and the United States.

San Antonio NORML has had a presence since 2011. We are a 501(c)3 nonprofit and we are completely volunteer run. All membership dues and donations go toward furthering the movement to make sure the citizens have safe and legal access to whole plant cannabis. As a chapter of the National Organization for the Reform of Marijuana Laws in the 7th largest city in the country, we work closely with our legislators and the state chapter of NORML to make sure the public opinion is being considered when making laws that affect us all. Our purpose is to educate and mobilize citizens who have been affected directly or indirectly by the war on drugs.

Action Items

In the state of Texas, the people do not have the ability to vote on drug policy at a local level. Every two years, the elected officials meet at the Capital to write bills, get feedback from constituents, listen to testimony from supporters/opposers of the bills and then vote on bills in the House and the Senate and then finally to pass them.
